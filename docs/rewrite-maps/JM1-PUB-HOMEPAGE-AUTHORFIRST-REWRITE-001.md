## 1. Purpose

This rewrite map defines the approved author-first / family-first homepage repositioning plan for J Merrill Publishing. It exists to guide implementation before any code-level homepage redesign begins.

This document applies the governing doctrine in [JM1-WEB-WHYFIRST-DOCTRINE-v1.0](/Users/jmerrillone/Developer/jmerrill-pub/docs/doctrine/JM1-WEB-WHYFIRST-DOCTRINE-v1.0.md): the homepage must lead with the author's Why, not the operator's system.

The homepage should make an author feel:
- understood
- protected
- professionally guided
- invited into a publishing family

It should not lead with:
- Publishing Operating System
- Division 01
- enterprise infrastructure
- Dataverse
- AI orchestration
- JM1 operating system
- platform roadmap

Those themes may live on deeper pages such as `/platform`, but not at the front door.

---

## 2. Core Homepage Promise

J Merrill Publishing helps authors protect, honor, and share their life’s work through professional publishing guidance, design, distribution, and long-term support.

Tone target:
- warm
- confident
- professional
- author-centered
- family-first

Approved public proof points:
- `125+ titles`
- five official imprints
- global distribution through Ingram’s retail and library network
- registered publisher
- author ownership / retained rights
- guided publishing process

Avoid leading the homepage with:
- `45,000+` endpoints
- Dataverse
- Azure AI
- AI orchestration
- operating system
- `95+ services` as a lead claim

---

## 3. Homepage Structure

The homepage will be rebuilt around this eight-section structure:

1. Hero
2. Choose Your Path
3. Why Authors Choose JMP
4. How Publishing Works
5. Find Your Publishing Path
6. Publishing Packages
7. Featured Books
8. Final CTA

---

## 4. Section Rewrite Map

### 4.1 Hero

**Purpose**  
Establish the emotional promise of the homepage immediately. The hero must tell authors what JMP believes about their work and why that belief matters.

**Visitor intent**  
An author is trying to decide whether JMP feels trustworthy, professional, and aligned with their need to keep ownership of their work.

**Current issue**  
The current homepage opens with impressive capability and system language instead of the author’s emotional stake. The visitor meets infrastructure before belief.

**New author-facing promise**  
Your words matter. Your legacy matters. Your rights matter. JMP exists to help you publish professionally without losing ownership of what you created.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Lead with a strong ownership-and-legacy headline. Support it with a warm subline about professional guidance, family-first support, and long-term partnership.

**CTA direction**  
Primary CTA should invite the author to begin the publishing journey. Secondary CTA may offer a lower-pressure discovery path such as exploring books or learning how publishing works.

**Content/components to keep**  
- strong premium visual presentation
- high-quality typography
- primary/secondary CTA pattern

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- Publishing Operating System framing
- Division 01 framing
- enterprise/system-first language
- technical capability proof points that belong deeper in the site

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Rebuild hero copy around belief, ownership, legacy, and guidance
- Preserve premium visual quality while removing operator-first language
- Keep CTA structure simple and high-confidence

---

### 4.2 Choose Your Path

**Purpose**  
Sort author intent quickly without overwhelming the visitor.

**Visitor intent**  
The visitor wants to know where to go next based on whether they want to publish, browse books, or access author resources.

**Current issue**  
The homepage has historically exposed too many pathways too early or used internal architecture logic instead of clean visitor pathways.

**New author-facing promise**  
We can meet you where you are and guide you to the right next step.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Use three simple, human choices only:
- I want to publish my book
- I want to explore the books
- I’m already a JMP author

**CTA direction**  
Each path should lead directly into a clean next step without additional explanation overhead.

**Content/components to keep**  
- existing card-based path selection patterns if visually strong

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- over-granular path splitting
- architecture rationale
- internal site-structure logic

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Keep this section visually simple
- Limit choices to three
- Make the publishing option dominant

---

### 4.3 Why Authors Choose JMP

**Purpose**  
Provide the trust proof that supports the emotional promise made in the hero.

**Visitor intent**  
The visitor wants evidence that JMP is real, established, and worthy of trust.

**Current issue**  
Some proof language has leaned too technical, too operational, or too fragile in its phrasing.

**New author-facing promise**  
Authors choose JMP because it combines professional publishing support with retained rights, a proven catalog, official imprints, and real distribution reach.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Frame proof points around trust:
- `125+ titles`
- five official imprints
- global distribution through Ingram’s retail and library network
- registered publisher
- retained rights / author ownership

**CTA direction**  
Optional soft CTA toward `/publishing` or `/about`, but the main function is proof, not action.

**Content/components to keep**  
- credibility/stat-row presentation
- imprint and catalog proof

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- `45,000+` endpoint language
- raw infrastructure language
- system bragging framed as visitor value

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Make proof points read as reasons to trust, not system specs
- Ensure `125+ titles` is the homepage standard

---

### 4.4 How Publishing Works

**Purpose**  
Make the process feel understandable, guided, and non-intimidating.

**Visitor intent**  
The author wants to know what working with JMP feels like and whether the process is supportive.

**Current issue**  
Process explanations have been too fragmented, too operational, or too system-centric.

**New author-facing promise**  
JMP guides authors through a professional publishing journey from discovery to release and beyond.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Present a clean, human sequence such as:
- Discover
- Edit
- Design
- Distribute
- Grow

Use plain-language explanations focused on care, clarity, and progress.

**CTA direction**  
Direct readers toward the publishing pathfinder or `/publishing`.

**Content/components to keep**  
- process/journey visuals
- milestone-style storytelling

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- duplicate process systems
- technical publishing infrastructure details
- overly granular operational diagrams

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Merge process explanation into one unified story
- Keep copy concise and human

---

### 4.5 Find Your Publishing Path

**Purpose**  
Help authors identify their best-fit starting point without turning the homepage into a diagnostic dashboard.

**Visitor intent**  
The author wants light guidance about what kind of publishing support they need.

**Current issue**  
Guidance tooling can drift into system-thinking or overly technical classification language.

**New author-facing promise**  
JMP can help you find the right publishing direction based on what you’re writing and what you want your book to do.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Use warm discovery language:
- What are you writing?
- What do you hope your book will do?
- Let’s help you find the right path

**CTA direction**  
Primary action should open the pathfinder or move the visitor toward `/join`.

**Content/components to keep**  
- any strong discovery/pathfinder component

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- system-heavy analyzer framing
- AI-first framing
- score-driven language that feels clinical

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Treat this as a discernment tool, not a tech demo
- Keep the copy author-centered, not machine-centered

---

### 4.6 Publishing Packages

**Purpose**  
Show clear publishing options without making the homepage feel like a pricing sheet.

**Visitor intent**  
The visitor wants to understand what levels of publishing support exist and whether JMP has an option that fits their needs.

**Current issue**  
Packages can dominate the narrative too early if they appear before trust and process are established.

**New author-facing promise**  
JMP offers clear publishing pathways with professional support, while still preserving the author’s ownership and long-term goals.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Lead with fit and guidance rather than transactional detail. Package framing should feel supportive and confidence-building.

**CTA direction**  
Each package card should move users toward `/packages`, `/join`, or a consultation path depending on the approved site pattern.

**Content/components to keep**  
- three-tier package structure
- existing card system

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- overly dominant package-first framing
- dense technical production detail better suited to deeper package pages

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Keep pricing logic untouched in this planning document
- Homepage package copy should summarize, not exhaust

---

### 4.7 Featured Books

**Purpose**  
Show living proof that authors trust JMP and that real books exist beyond the promise.

**Visitor intent**  
The visitor wants to see examples of the catalog and imagine their work belonging among it.

**Current issue**  
Catalog presentation can drift into a data-table feeling or overemphasize count instead of meaning.

**New author-facing promise**  
JMP is already home to a growing library of published authors and books across multiple imprints and genres.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Frame the section as a family gallery or curated catalog proof point. The catalog should feel alive, not merely counted.

**CTA direction**  
Primary action should be `View full catalog`.

**Content/components to keep**  
- featured book grid
- imprint visibility
- link to `/books`

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- overly technical sorting logic in visible copy
- data-heavy framing that weakens the emotional effect

**Codex implementation notes**  
- Preserve the `125+` public standard in homepage-facing catalog copy
- Keep filtered or deeper catalog logic on `/books`

---

### 4.8 Final CTA

**Purpose**  
Convert belief, trust, and understanding into one warm next step.

**Visitor intent**  
The author is ready to reach out, ask questions, or begin the publishing relationship.

**Current issue**  
If the homepage closes too technically or too generically, it weakens the emotional journey built above.

**New author-facing promise**  
You do not have to figure this out alone. JMP is ready to hear your story and help you take the next step.

**Recommended copy direction**  
Use invitation language such as:
- Tell Us About Your Book
- Talk With Our Publishing Team
- Start Your Publishing Journey

**CTA direction**  
Primary CTA should point toward `/join`. Secondary CTA may offer consultation scheduling if approved in the final build.

**Content/components to keep**  
- strong closing CTA section
- premium visual close

**Content/components to remove or relocate**  
- platform/product-system language
- roadmap or future-state language

**Codex implementation notes**  
- End with warmth, clarity, and confidence
- The close should feel like invitation, not routing

## 7. Codex Implementation Summary

When implementation begins, Codex should:
- preserve the premium visual quality of the homepage
- reorganize the page around the eight approved sections
- remove operator-first language from the public front door
- lead with ownership, trust, guidance, and family-first publishing care
- keep proof points stable and human-readable
- treat the homepage as an author-conversion surface, not a systems showcase

The homepage should answer this question better than it does now:

> Can I trust J Merrill Publishing with my life’s work?

If a section does not strengthen that answer, it should be rewritten, moved deeper, or removed.
