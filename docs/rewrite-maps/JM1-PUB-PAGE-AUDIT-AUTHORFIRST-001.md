**Proving instance:** J Merrill Publishing, Inc. — `jmerrill.pub`

## 1. Purpose

This document audits the remaining public jmerrill.pub pages against the approved Why-First Doctrine and the author-first homepage standard now established under `JM1-PUB-HOMEPAGE-AUTHORFIRST-REWRITE-001`.

Its purpose is to define what each page should promise, what should be kept, what should be rewritten, what should be moved deeper, and what should be quarantined to technical or partner-facing surfaces before the next page-by-page implementation work begins.

This is a planning and governance document only. It does not modify site code, integrations, pricing logic, data sources, or intake behavior.

Doctrine test applied to every page:
- Why would an author visit this page?
- What fear, hope, or decision does this page address?
- Does the page speak to the author, or explain the company to itself?
- Does it make JMP feel more trustworthy?
- Does it clearly move the visitor toward joining the family?
- Is technology visible only where it strengthens confidence?
- Is the next step obvious?

## 5. Phase 1 — Author-Acquisition Core

### 5.1 `/about`

**Page route**  
`/about`

**Current page purpose**  
Explain who JMP is, what it believes, and how it fits inside the broader JM1 context.

**Primary visitor intent**  
An author wants to know whether JMP is a trustworthy publishing house with the right values, philosophy, and relationship model.

**Current issue**  
The page starts well, but drifts into company/system explanation, numeric stats, and JM1 enterprise framing too early. It risks becoming an identity dump instead of a trust page.

**Author-first promise the page should make**  
JMP exists because authors deserve a publishing home that protects their rights, honors their message, and helps their work endure.

**Content to keep**  
- opening line about authors deserving a publisher that believes in them
- mission language around empowerment and publishing home
- ownership emphasis
- family culture / transparency / legacy principles

**Content to rewrite**  
- technology-forward mission paragraph
- numeric stats section if it reads as self-description rather than author reassurance
- division/system identity rows that explain the company to itself

**Content to move deeper or quarantine**  
- `Division 01`
- `Parent System`
- enterprise-layer framing
- JM1 system explanation

**Recommended CTA**  
`Tell Us About Your Book`

**Priority level**  
`P1`

**Codex implementation notes**  
Refocus the page around why JMP exists, what authors keep, what authors gain, and why the relationship is different from generic publishing vendors.

**Risk notes**  
If the JM1 system section remains too prominent, the page will undermine the homepage doctrine by redirecting trust into infrastructure too quickly.

`/publishing`

- “future-ready architecture”
- system/platform explanation of services

**Content to move deeper or quarantine**  
- Dataverse support language
- CRM/workflow readiness language
- platform integration implications

**Recommended CTA**  
`Tell Us About Your Book`

**Priority level**  
`P1`

**Codex implementation notes**  
This should become the definitive “What does publishing with JMP feel like?” page in author language. It should support the homepage, not compete with `/platform`.

If this page stays architecture-heavy, it will confuse authors who arrive after the warmer homepage and expect a human publishing explanation.

### 5.4 `/packages`

**Page route**  
`/packages`

**Current page purpose**  
Show structured publishing packages, pricing, flexible payment plans, and detailed feature comparison.

**Primary visitor intent**  
An author wants to understand which publishing package fits their manuscript, goals, and budget.

**Current issue**  
The page is commercially useful, but it still behaves mainly as a feature/pricing matrix rather than a fit-and-guidance page.

**Author-first promise the page should make**  
JMP offers clear publishing paths so authors can choose the right level of support without guesswork or hidden complexity.

**Content to keep**

- pricing
- payment plans
- comparison matrix
- Ghostwriting + Publishing Bundle callout

**Content to rewrite**  
- hero/supporting copy so it emphasizes fit, guidance, and professional outcomes
- package card summaries so they explain who each package is for
- CTA labels so they feel more exploratory and confidence-building

**Content to move deeper or quarantine**  
- overly dense production-feature language that belongs in expand/collapse or comparison sections

**Recommended CTA**  
- Starter: `Explore Starter`
- Professional: `Explore Professional`
- Signature: `Apply for Signature`

**Priority level**  
`P1`

**Codex implementation notes**  
Signature should be framed as selective/prestige rather than just the most expensive option. Fit language matters more than matrix density at the top of the page.

**Risk notes**  
If this page stays price-first, authors may sort themselves purely by budget instead of by publishing need and long-term goals.

## 7. Phase 3 — Technical Quarantine

### 7.1 `/platform`

**Page route**  
`/platform`

**Current page purpose**  
Explain the future publishing stack, enterprise roadmap, and how the web layer will connect to Dataverse, Dynamics, BI, and governed AI.

**Primary visitor intent**  
This page is for partners, stakeholders, and advanced authors who want to understand the infrastructure and strategic direction behind the publishing system.

**Current issue**  
This is the correct location for technical/system language, but it should be clearer about who the page is for and why they would care.

**Author-first promise the page should make**  
For readers who want the deeper system story, this page shows how JMP is building durable publishing infrastructure without compromising the human publishing experience.

**Content to keep**  
- Dataverse
- Dynamics
- Power BI
- AI-driven operations
- roadmap modules
- implementation-phase framing

**Content to rewrite**  
- only as needed to clarify intended audience and purpose
- ensure this page explicitly reads as behind-the-scenes or advanced infrastructure context

**Content to move deeper or quarantine**  
- nothing major; this page is already the quarantine destination

**Recommended CTA**  
`Return to Home` or `See the author journey`

**Priority level**  
`P3`

**Codex implementation notes**  
This page should remain more technical than the rest of the site. The main requirement is audience clarity: it is not the front door.

**Risk notes**  
If `/platform` is softened too far, the site loses its proper technical quarantine surface. If it remains but is poorly explained, visitors may still stumble into it without context.

## 8. Recommended Rewrite Sequence

Recommended order of implementation:

1. `/publishing`
2. `/about`
3. `/packages`
4. `/author-journey`
5. `/services`
6. `/publishing-partner`
7. `/authors`
8. `/books`
9. `/distribution`
10. `/memberships`
11. `/platform`

Why this sequence:
- `/publishing`, `/about`, `/packages`, and `/author-journey` are the highest-trust decision pages in the author-acquisition funnel.
- `/services` and `/publishing-partner` shape capability and premium-path understanding.
- `/authors`, `/books`, and `/distribution` act as proof and reassurance after intent is established.
- `/memberships` matters after the core trust architecture is corrected.
- `/platform` is already the correct technical home and can be refined last.
