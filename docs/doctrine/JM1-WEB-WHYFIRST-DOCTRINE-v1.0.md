## 1. Purpose

This doctrine establishes how every J Merrill One public web property speaks to the people it serves. It exists because a review of jmerrill.pub uncovered a systemic concern: the site was authored from the builder's point of view, not the visitor's. The doctrine corrects that — enterprise-wide — and gives every future page, on every division site, a fixed standard to be built and measured against.

It is the web expression of the JM1 Human-First Operating Principle. The principle says people are the reason for the work. This doctrine makes the websites prove it.

---

## 2. The Finding

jmerrill.pub is technically excellent and an accurate map of the system behind it. That is the problem. It leads with **What** — catalog, packages, service breadth — and with **How the machine works** — Dataverse, Azure AI, the operating-system framing, the platform roadmap, the site architecture itself. It is a *What-first* site, pointed at the operator who built it.

The visitor it needs to win is not asking what infrastructure runs underneath. The author standing outside with a manuscript is asking one question, and it is a **Why** question:

> *Can I trust this publisher with what took me years to write — and will they help my message reach people, without taking it from me?*

The finding is not "too much content." The content is ordered around **capability** instead of **calling**. Every JM1 site has the same risk, because every JM1 site was built by the same hands. This doctrine is the correction, applied once and reused across the board.

---

## 3. The Governing Question

Every JM1 public page is measured against one sentence. For Publishing it reads:

> **Does this page help an author trust J Merrill Publishing with their life's work?**

If the answer is no, the page is rewritten, moved deeper, or removed from the primary visitor journey. Each division states the same question in its own terms (Section 10).

---

## 4. The Why-First Spine

Every JM1 site tells its story in Golden Circle order: **Why → How → What.** Belief first. Method second. Proof third.

### 4.1 The JMP Spine

**WHY — the belief.**
You wrote something that matters. It carries your story, your message, your name. J Merrill Publishing exists because work like that deserves to be published *professionally* — and to *stay yours*. Not gatekept until rejected. Not absorbed into someone else's catalog. Yours, and built to last.

**HOW — the way we keep that promise.**
A guided publishing family that walks the whole road with the author — editorial, design, distribution, and long-term support — bringing the infrastructure of a major house while the author keeps the rights and the legacy. *Your name is on the front. Ours is on the back. We are here to support you.*

**WHAT — the proof.**
Five imprints. 125+ titles in the world. Professional editorial and design. Global distribution through Ingram. A path that continues after launch.

The Why is not new. It already lives in "Helping authors help themselves" and "Your words. Your legacy. Your rights." The site simply buried it. This doctrine brings it back to the front door.

### 4.2 The Competitive Why — Why Not the Big Five

Traditional major publishing often makes authors feel they must choose between being taken seriously and keeping ownership of their work. J Merrill Publishing exists to challenge that tradeoff — professional standards and retained rights, in one house. This contrast is the competitive Why. It belongs on author-facing pages (`/about`, `/publishing`) in the author's language — never as a technical comparison, always as a question of trust, stewardship, and ownership.

## 5. The Three-Altitude Test

The doctrine is enforced at three altitudes. Together they are complete: one tests the page, one tests how to build the page, one tests a single line.

### 5.1 Altitude 1 — The Governing Question *(page level)*

The single sentence in Section 3. The first and broadest filter.

### 5.2 Altitude 2 — The Family-First Page Test *(page-build level)*

Every JM1 public page must:

1. **Open with Why** — the visitor's stake (their legacy, their story, their reach) — before any What (catalog, mechanics, infrastructure).
2. **Speak to the visitor, not the system** — internal vocabulary is translated, never published raw (Section 7).
3. **Make the visitor feel chosen and cared for** — never tested, never sorted into a commercial tier, never made to pass an entrance exam.
4. **Lead to one clear next step** toward joining.
5. **Quarantine technical and platform language** to explicitly internal or partner-facing surfaces.

### 5.3 Altitude 3 — The Blind Gear Principle *(line / component level)*

> If a feature or a piece of copy explains *how the system works technically* rather than *how it protects, honors, or elevates the person we serve*, it must be removed from the public eye. The complexity belongs in the Dataverse schemas and `jm1_executionlog`. The warmth belongs on the web.

The gears are real, and they are good. They run silently so the experience feels seamless. The visitor never sees them turn.

## 6. Messaging Hierarchy — Lead With Why

Public messaging moves in this order, on the site overall and within each page:

1. Why we exist
2. Who we serve
3. What we protect
4. How we guide them
5. What the journey looks like
6. What packages and services are available
7. What proof we have
8. How to join

It does **not** move: platform → system → architecture → automation → intelligence → services → invitation. That order is technically impressive and emotionally backward.

*Note for the build:* this hierarchy is the narrative order of the story, not a literal section list. The locked 8-section homepage (Section 11.1) is the layout; this hierarchy governs the order the Why-to-What story is told within and across those sections.

## 8. Tone Vocabulary

The site should feel like a warm, face-to-face consultation in a confident, high-end house — not a dashboard. Internal tone references: the *Entryway* (landing), the *Living Room* (where an author finds where they belong), the *Kitchen Table* (the publishing process explained in human steps), the *Family Gallery* (the catalog as a living library of authors who trusted us, not a data table).

**Guardrail:** these metaphors guide *tone* and may guide *internal* section naming. They are not literal public headings. Public headings stay clean and direct — "How publishing works," never "The Kitchen Table." Warmth, not whimsy.

---

## 9. The Per-Page Audit — Seven Author Trust Questions

Every existing page is audited against these before it is rewritten:

1. Why would a visitor come to this page?
2. What fear, hope, or decision does this page address?
3. Does the page speak to the visitor, or explain the company to itself?
4. Does it make JMP feel more trustworthy?
5. Does it clearly move the visitor toward joining the family?
6. Is the technology visible only where it strengthens confidence?
7. Is the next step obvious?

## 10. The JM1 Brand Fork

One doctrine, one spine, one three-altitude test, one copy discipline. Each JM1 branch supplies its own Why and its own governing question. JMP is the proving instance; the remaining JM1 branches fork from it.

**J Merrill Publishing — Family-First.**
*Why:* work that carries someone's story and name deserves to be published professionally and to stay theirs.
*Governing question:* does this page help an author trust JMP with their life's work?
*Back of the house:* Dataverse author graph, AI publishing layer, platform roadmap → `/platform`.

**J Merrill Financial — Cared-For and Protected.**
*Why:* families should never face loss, incapacity, or legacy decisions in confusion or fear. JMF exists so families are prepared, guided, and protected. *It's not about me — it's about our families.*
*Governing question:* does this page help a family feel cared for and protected from confusion?
*Back of the house:* SKU matrices, Dataverse stages, Power Automate flows → internal/governed surfaces.

**J Merrill Foundation — Dignity.**
*Why:* community members deserve dignity, respect, and practical support — never to feel like a case number.
*Governing question:* does this page treat a community member with dignity?
*Back of the house:* constituent schema, grant infrastructure → internal surfaces.

**J Merrill Productions — Stories Honored.**
*Why:* stories and the creators behind them deserve to be honored, not processed.
*Governing question:* does this page make a creator feel their story is honored?
*Back of the house:* rights management tables, production pipeline → internal surfaces.

## 11. The Author-First Rewrite Map — JMP Proving Instance

### 11.1 Homepage — Locked 8-Section Structure

1. **Hero** — "Your words. Your legacy. Your rights." A warm value line ("a publishing family that protects, honors, and shares your voice"). One dominant CTA, one quiet secondary. No "Operating System," no "enterprise," no "Division 01."
2. **Choose Your Path** — intent-sort doorway, three options only: *I want to publish my book* (dominant) · *I want to explore the books* · *I'm already a JMP author*. Three, not seven — finer-grained intent belongs in the Pathfinder.
3. **Why authors choose JMP** — the trust beat. The single reconciled **125+ titles** count lives here, with years in business and Ingram reach, in author-ownership language.
4. **How publishing works** — one merged journey (Discover · Edit · Design · Distribute · Grow), replacing all three current process diagrams.
5. **Find Your Publishing Path** — the Pathfinder tool, carrying the warm "what are you writing?" tone; this is where finer-grained intent and genre live.
6. **Publishing Packages** — three tiers, guided CTAs; Signature positioned as publisher-selected prestige, never a commercial top tier.
7. **Featured Books** — six titles, link to the full catalog. The homepage is not the catalog.
8. **Final CTA** — warm invitation language ("Tell Us About Your Book" / "Talk With Our Publishing Team").

System-heavy content is translated per Section 7 and relocated to existing pages — no new routes. The marquee and the "Multi-Page Flagship Architecture" section are removed (see the hygiene-pass spec, JM1-PUB-WEB-HYGIENE-001).

### 11.2 Page Rollout Phases

- **Phase 1 — Homepage.** Built first, against this doctrine, as the proof instance.
- **Phase 2 — Author-acquisition core.** `/about`, `/publishing`, `/services`, `/packages`, `/author-journey`, `/publishing-partner`. Highest Why-leverage.
- **Phase 3 — Proof and retention.** `/books`, `/authors`, `/distribution`, `/memberships`.
- **Phase 4 — Technical quarantine.** Confirm `/platform` proudly owns the full JM1 system, Dataverse, and AI story for partners and stakeholders, and that no internal vocabulary leaks onto any author-facing page.

Each page is mapped before code: purpose, visitor intent, current issue, new promise, sections to keep / rewrite / move, new CTA, Chad build notes, Codex review notes.
