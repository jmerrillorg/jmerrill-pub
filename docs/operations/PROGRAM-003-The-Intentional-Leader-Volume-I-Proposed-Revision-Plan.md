# The Intentional Leader - Volume I Proposed Revision Plan

## Revision Priorities

### Priority 1 - Preserve and sharpen the quarterly arc

- Keep January as foundation and alignment.
- Keep February as interior deepening.
- Keep March as activation and readiness.
- Add clearer bridge language at the January/February and February/March boundaries.

### Priority 2 - Rebalance length and pacing

- Expand the shortest January entries so they carry equal devotional weight.
- Trim or streamline the longest late-February entries where repetition slows pace.
- Keep March energetic without allowing concept overlap to crowd the final run.

### Priority 3 - Differentiate recurring leadership concepts

- Make each recurrence of clarity, attention, trust, alignment, courage, stewardship, and wisdom serve a distinct emotional or spiritual function.
- Prevent late-February and late-March clusters from sounding like parallel versions of the same exhortation.

### Priority 4 - Strengthen quarter-end closure

- Treat March 28-31 as the quarter's resolution sequence.
- Make completion, threshold, and bridge language more intentional so Volume I ends with rest and commissioning rather than drift.

## Deliverable Outcome

After revision, Volume I should read as a complete devotional quarter with consistent daily rhythm, a more controlled emotional crescendo, and a closing movement that naturally invites Volume II.
