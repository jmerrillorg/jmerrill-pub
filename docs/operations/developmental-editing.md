**Version:** v1.0-operational-proof
**Effective date:** 2026-07-11
**Supersession:** replaces thin developmental stubs for active implementation purposes; future substantive changes require version increment and synchronized publication
**Applies to:** PROGRAM-003 Developmental Editing stage inside the Editorial Command Center
**Current controlled proof title:** *The Intentional Leader* - Volume I

## 1. Purpose

Developmental Editing exists to strengthen the manuscript at the structural, pacing, continuity, thematic, and reader-experience level without replacing the author's voice or collapsing the work into line editing.

## 2. Scope

This stage governs the integrated Developmental Review Package:

- Editorial Letter
- Editorial Working Manuscript
- Revision Blueprint
- Publisher Recommendation
- Cross-Reference Map
- Internal Evidence
- GPAT / model-provenance metadata

## 3. Entry Criteria

Developmental Editing may begin only when:

- Editorial Review is complete
- the approved source manuscript is registered
- the publishing asset, title, relationship, stage, and gate exist
- upstream imprint and style-guide inheritance is settled
- payment and relationship readiness are satisfied where applicable
- the stage transition is authorized

## 4. Exit Criteria

Developmental Editing exits only when:

- the author decision is captured truthfully
- required revisions are received, waived, or held by approved exception
- the Publisher Recommendation is settled
- next-stage authorization is recorded
- evidence is retained and versioned

## 5. Author Experience

The author receives a strengths-first, navigable, manuscript-backed package. The experience must explain the manuscript opportunity before asking for a decision and must never lead with internal process language.

## 6. Integrated Package Standard

Developmental Editing is one integrated editorial experience, not a loose collection of unrelated files. The Letter, Working Manuscript, Blueprint, and Recommendation must cross-reference each other through stable identifiers.

## 7. Editorial Letter Requirements

The Editorial Letter must:

- begin with strengths and manuscript potential
- explain the quarter or book-level arc
- identify material developmental opportunities
- direct the reviewer into manuscript-level evidence
- use stable section anchors
- remain diagnostic rather than line-level

## 8. Editorial Working Manuscript Requirements

The Working Manuscript must:

- preserve source-manuscript lineage
- use stable anchors stronger than page numbers alone
- support diagnostic comments, author questions, and optional examples
- identify scope-boundary violations instead of silently rewriting them away
- maintain version-aware linkage to the Letter and Blueprint

## 9. Revision Blueprint Requirements

The Revision Blueprint must translate the developmental findings into ordered actions with:

- priority
- rationale
- dependencies
- linked evidence
- author-decision markers where applicable

## 10. Publisher Recommendation Requirements

The Publisher Recommendation is a first-class governed component. It must record a supported disposition without automatically advancing the stage.

Supported dispositions include:

- Proceed with Developmental Revisions
- Author Consultation Required
- Structural Revision Required
- Additional Developmental Pass Required
- Approved for Author Review
- Ready for Line Editing
- Ready for Copy Editing where appropriate
- Hold for Publisher Review
- Pause

## 11. Cross-Reference Standard

Cross-references must be:

- bidirectional where material
- stable within the package version
- human-readable
- integrity-checked
- version-aware

## 12. Diagnostic-Only Boundary

Developmental Editing may question, diagnose, prioritize, flag, and offer options. It may not default to:

- sentence rewriting
- prose polishing
- copyediting
- proofreading
- autonomous replacement of author voice

## 13. Style-Guide and Imprint Inheritance

Developmental Editing inherits approved upstream values for:

- imprint
- manuscript type
- category or path
- audience
- primary style guide
- companion guides
- approved source manuscript

These values are inherited, not re-derived.

The JMP Project Style Sheet initializes during Editorial Review and follows the manuscript through:

`Editorial Review -> Developmental -> Line -> Copy -> Proof -> Archive`

It is an internal project layer, not one of the 14 external guides.

## 14. Author-Voice Protection

The author's cadence, devotional tone, and stated purpose must remain primary. Developmental guidance may strengthen the delivery of that voice but may not flatten it into generic publisher prose.

## 15. Human Review

Human review is mandatory before:

- package approval
- author release
- gate closure
- stage advancement
- publisher recommendation finalization

## 16. Publisher Approval

Publisher approval is required for:

- release-ready package disposition
- override of diagnostic-boundary violations
- material exception handling
- final stage advancement out of Developmental Editing

## 17. GPAT-003 Relationship

GPAT-003 governs the Developmental Review Package as an implementation-in-progress runtime capability. GPAT assistance may support analysis and drafting, but GPAT does not approve or release the package.

## 18. Model Provenance

Each governed package retains provenance references for:

- GPAT ID and version
- provider and deployment route
- prompt key and version
- inherited style-guide set
- human reviewer
- publisher approver

## 18A. Scripture and Readability

- Scripture handling remains translation-aware with KJV as the house baseline.
- Non-KJV quotation requires explicit publisher direction and rights awareness.
- Readability is governed by method, not by hard-coded Lexile alone.
- Supported readability methods include Lexile, grade level, named provider, score, assessment date, and vocabulary rulings.

## 19. Delivery Standard

Email remains the official transactional channel for author decisions. The Author Operating Center may mirror truthful status only when the released artifact exists and is author-accessible.

## 20. Author Response Handling

Author responses must map to the correct package version and approval gate. No later package or stage may overwrite the recorded decision for the current package.

## 20A. Correction Count Standard

Mechanical correction count must be deterministic and report:

- recurring rule
- corrected instances
- preserved voice
- preserved dialect
- preserved cadence

Author-safe summary form:

`We corrected X mechanical instances across Y recurring editorial patterns while intentionally preserving the manuscript's voice.`

## 21. Revision Receipt

Author revisions must be tied back to the package version, the approved manuscript lineage, and the next truthful stage state.

## 22. Downstream Stage Transitions

Developmental Editing may lead to:

- continued Developmental Editing
- another developmental pass
- line editing
- hold or publisher review

No downstream stage begins from AI output alone.

## 23. Execution Events

Truthful execution evidence includes:

- DEVELOPMENTAL_PACKAGE_CREATED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_PACKAGE_ASSEMBLY_STARTED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_PACKAGE_INTERNAL_REVIEW_STARTED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_PACKAGE_INTERNAL_REVIEW_COMPLETED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_PACKAGE_APPROVED_FOR_AUTHOR_RELEASE
- DEVELOPMENTAL_AUTHOR_PACKAGE_RELEASED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_EDITORIAL_LETTER_COMPLETED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_WORKING_MANUSCRIPT_COMPLETED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_REVISION_BLUEPRINT_COMPLETED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_PUBLISHER_RECOMMENDATION_RECORDED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_CROSSREF_VALIDATION_COMPLETED
- DEVELOPMENTAL_DIAGNOSTIC_SCOPE_ATTESTED

## 24. Evidence Retention

Substantive package evidence belongs in governed SharePoint. Operational truth and references belong in JM1-Core. Superseded packages remain retained for lineage and audit.

## 25. Exceptions and Overrides

Material scope-boundary violations, broken anchors, or approval anomalies require an explicit override reason and governed evidence. Silence is not an override.

## 26. Supersession / Versioning

Only one current package version may be active for a stage at a time. Superseded versions remain available for reference but may not compete with the current governed package.
